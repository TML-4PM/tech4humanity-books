# Tier 1 Story Template (25,000 words)

**Use for:** Foundation stories. Establishes core governance principles, sets technical standards referenced by other vignettes, contains regulatory blueprints legislators can use.

**Foundation stories (8):** #1 Subscription Disconnect, #2 Cloud Custody Crisis, #6 Healthcare Network Failure, #9 DeepFake Democracy, #14 Algorithmic Unemployment Wave, #15 Biometric Surveillance, #20 Climate Model Corruption, #27 Standards War Stalemate, #32 Governance Compact (capstone)

---

## Part 1 — The Event (~4,500 words)

**Purpose:** Cinematic crisis told from 3–5 viewpoints in parallel.

- **Multi-location narrative** (4–5 parallel storylines, geographic and demographic spread)
- **Hour-by-hour timeline** (12 hours of crisis compressed into vivid scenes)
- **Technical forensics** with architecture detail (what specifically broke)
- **Economic & human impact** ($X affected, Y critical services down, Z lives at risk)

**Voice:** Cinematic present tense, intimate third person. Mix of action and reflection. Sensory specificity.

## Part 2 — Stakeholder Universe (~7,000 words)

**Purpose:** Show how the same event lands across 5 stakeholder groups.

- **Government & Regulators** (~1,400 words) — political response, legislative drafting
- **Industry** (~1,400 words) — corporate response, internal conflict, financial impact
- **Standards Bodies** (~1,400 words) — emergency working groups, technical standards drafting
- **Civil Society** (~1,400 words) — community organising, advocacy, victim mobilisation
- **Public** (~1,400 words) — social media, fragmented reactions, cultural shift

**Voice:** Documentary-style. Each section a self-contained scene with named people, places, decisions.

## Part 3 — GC-BAT System Response (~8,500 words)

**Purpose:** GC-BAT analyses crisis and produces governance framework.

### Marcus Bennett opens (~800 words)
- What happened (factual synthesis, no judgment)
- Which unit(s) lead the response
- Three questions: *What failed? Who is harmed? What is the fix?*
- Delegates to units; sets timeline

### Holo-ORG (Sofia Ruiz) — Systems Architecture (~1,700 words)
- Failure mode analysis (single points, cascade triggers, recovery blockers)
- Architecture diagrams (described in prose)
- Technical solutions: redundancy, fail-safe, interoperability, holonic design
- Cost-benefit numbers (implementation vs failure cost)

### NeuroPAK (Kwame Adeyemi) — Cognitive & Psychological (~1,700 words)
- Mental health impact measurement (acute, short-term, long-term)
- Human-centred solutions (UX, graceful degradation, transparent comms)
- Dependency-recognition framework
- Recovery pathways

### RATPAK (Col. Sarah Mitchell) — Physical & Operational (~1,700 words)
- Field deployment data (where they went, what they found)
- Operational gaps (response time, coordination, analog readiness)
- Resilience protocols (backup systems, drills, pre-positioning)
- Priority classification system

### AHC (Elijah Foster) — Equity & Human Outcomes (~1,800 words)
- Disproportionate harm analysis (elderly, disabled, low-income, rural, immigrant)
- Restoration equity (who gets back online first, and why)
- Accessibility mandates
- Community resilience programs

### Marcus synthesises (~600 words)
- Integrates four units into coherent strategy
- Prioritises immediate / short / long-term
- Names the systemic logic: incentives produce vulnerability
- Always closes: *This was preventable. Prevention is our job.*

## Part 4 — Roadmap & Futures (~5,000 words)

**Purpose:** Implementation pathway, scenario planning, accountability metrics.

- **24-month action plan** (~2,000 words) — Q-by-Q milestones: government, standards, industry, civil society actions per quarter
- **Three alternative futures** (~1,500 words total, ~500 each)
  - Best case: *The Resilient Commonwealth* (probability ~35%)
  - Middle case: *The Fragmented Patchwork* (probability ~45%)
  - Worst case: *The Recurring Crisis* (probability ~20%)
- **Metrics of success** (~1,000 words) — technical, social, equity, economic, qualitative
- **Final reflection** (~500 words) — closing statement from Marcus Bennett or the in-world framework document

---

## Style notes

- Tier 1 stories are policy documents that read like fiction. Both must work.
- Every technical claim should be defensible. Every emotional beat should be earned.
- End with sense of completion AND momentum to the next story.
- Reference characters from other stories to thread the universe. Sparingly.
- The Internal Response section follows the Marcus pattern *exactly* across all 32 stories — this is the spine of the project.
