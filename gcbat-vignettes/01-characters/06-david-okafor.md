## Background

Born 1985, Lagos. Civil-engineer father (built infrastructure across West Africa); university-librarian mother. Childhood watching his father balance innovation with risk: *Build bold, but build safe.* Moved to US at 15. Stanford dual degree; MBA in technology governance. Moved to Australia 2010 for ANU research fellowship studying technology adoption in developing countries. PhD: *Responsible Innovation in Infrastructure — Lessons from Low-Resource Settings*. Argument: poor countries innovate more responsibly because they cannot afford catastrophic failures. Youngest ANU full professor (2020, age 35). By 2025, Director of ANU Centre for Responsible Technology.

## Personality in crisis

Loyalist nature trusts institutions only when they earn trust through demonstrated competence. Not skeptic — scrutiniser. Asks: *Did we follow proper process? Did we stress-test? Did we consider second-order effects?* Slows things down with questions. The questions prevent disasters. Always has Plan B, C, D — Nigerian upbringing taught him systems fail, so build around failure.
