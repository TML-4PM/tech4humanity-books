# Marcus Bennett — GC-BAT Director

**Enneagram:** Type 8 (The Challenger) 
**Heritage:** African-American, Atlanta → Sydney 
**Education:** Harvard Law 2004 
**Stories:** All 32 (internal sections); public emergence in #32

## Background

Born 1982, Atlanta. Family descended from civil-rights activists — grandfather marched with MLK, father was voting-rights lawyer, mother taught African-American history. Grew up breathing justice. Harvard Law top 5%. Federal clerkship 2004–2006. Corporate law 2006–2010 specialising in financial regulation — saw 2008 crisis up close. SEC 2010–2014. Recruited to Australia 2014 to design corporate governance reforms after the banking royal commission. Australian citizen 2019. Founded **GC-BAT (Governance Compact for Beneficial AI & Technology)** in 2023 as independent think tank with strict funder firewall.

## Personality in crisis

Challenger nature confronts power directly — strategically. Not reckless revolutionary; principled operator. Asks *Who is accountable? What is the systematic fix? How do we prevent recurrence?* Moral clarity without moral superiority. Operates like chess: thinks five moves ahead, sacrifices pawns to protect the king (public interest).

## Role across all 32 stories

Marcus appears in **Part 3 (GC-BAT System Response)** of every story. He never appears in the cinematic scenarios (those are lived by the other eight) until Story #32, when he steps into public view as Compact co-convener.

## Internal Response pattern (template for every story)

1. **Marcus opens** (200–300 words) — synthesises what happened, identifies which units lead, asks: *What failed? Who is harmed? What is the fix?* Delegates.
2. **Each unit responds** (1,500–2,000 words each):
   - **Holo-ORG** (Sofia Ruiz) — systems architecture analysis
   - **NeuroPAK** (Kwame Adeyemi) — cognitive & psychological impact
   - **RATPAK** (Sarah Mitchell) — physical infrastructure & rapid deployment
   - **AHC** (Elijah Foster) — human outcomes & equity
3. **Marcus closes** (300–400 words) — integrates the four into coherent strategy; prioritises immediate/short/long-term actions; identifies policy recommendations. Always ends: *This was preventable. Prevention is our job. Let's do it.*

## Trajectory

- **#1–#10** — Reactive; analysing crises as they happen. GC-BAT builds credibility.
- **#11–#20** — Proactive; publishing warnings. Some heeded, some ignored. Frustration mounts.
- **#21–#31** — Strategic; maps systemic patterns. Begins behind-the-scenes work to organise the Compact summit.
- **#32** — Public emergence. Process architect of the Compact. Designs working groups, decision protocols, consensus mechanisms. Becomes first Executive Director of the International Technology Standards Secretariat (ITSS).
