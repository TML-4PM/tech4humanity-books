## Premise

A US Executive Order (EO 14118, BIS expansion of Entity List) directs US hyperscalers to implement enhanced compliance controls on Chinese-related workloads. As a side effect, the automated capacity-management systems of AWS, Azure, and Google Cloud silently deprioritise non-US workloads sharing the affected infrastructure. The Commonwealth of Australia's federal services — Centrelink, ATO, MyGov, Medicare, DFAT consular — degrade across the day. Provider status pages remain *all systems operational.* No actor breaches any contract. The Australian state cannot, operationally, perform its functions.

The failure mode is what David Okafor's 2023 paper called *silent capacity reallocation under foreign regulatory pressure.* The Senate inquiry vindicates the paper. The framework being built does not.

## Frameworks introduced (canon)

- **AS/NZS 9001:2026** — Cloud Sovereignty Assessment Framework (David Okafor's standard). Four dimensions: legal jurisdiction, operational control, substitution feasibility, continuity under adversarial conditions.
- **MJOC** — Multi-Jurisdictional Operational Continuity (Sofia Ruiz). Five principles: jurisdictional diversity by default, Australian operational layer, cryptographic sovereignty (Hold Your Own Key), substitution drills (Sovereign Substitution Day), continuity manifest.
- **CARE Principles adoption** (Elijah Foster + Maiam nayri Wingara) — Collective Benefit, Authority to Control, Responsibility, Ethics; adopted as equity foundation, not adaptation.
- **Custody to Capability** — GC-BAT framework. Four pillars: Jurisdictional Architecture, Institutional Frame, Operational Continuity, Equity Foundation. Cost: $4.8B over 5 years.
- **Commonwealth Cloud Operations Office (CCOO)** — standing vendor management capability, modelled on the JSF Programme Office.
- **Commonwealth Digital Incident Command** — standing cross-agency coordination structure for digital degradation events.
- **Quad Cloud Sovereignty Arrangement** (2026) and **ASEAN Data Sovereignty Framework** (2026) — international scaffolding.
